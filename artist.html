About Me

Mugire peace David is a mixed media artist whose works intricately explores the nuances of everyday life and the stunning landscapes of Rwanda. During his high schools he joined NYUNDO SCHOOL OF ARTS AND MUSIC in 2018 where he developed diverse art techniques, with a particular focus on ball pen “Once a mistake is made, it can’t be erased, and so you have to turn the error into some sort of design” he said once in an interview by The New Times 2021. From 2012 David participated and excelled in sevelar art competitions like Rwanda transparency art competition, BIC art master and Art Rwanda ubuhanzi, among others.

David’s innovative mixed media approach has garnered international recognition, leading to exhitions globally in different countries such as Belgium,France and USA. His unique perspective has also invited him to speak and share his experience at prestigious art academies such as Mechellen art academy and paperinge art academy in belgium. Throughout his career David has collaborated on significant projects with major NGOs such as UNHCR (Rwanda) and UNESCO (paris).

My source of inspirations is our everyday life experiences and African heritage” he uses to say. His art fact a tribute to Rwanda’s beautiful landscape and its hard working people, eternalized in his paintings and drawings, being able to tell real life stories and highlight the most important aspects of life within Rwandese society. Many of Mugire’s artworks were collected by different private collectors from U.S.A,U.K,France,Italy,Belgium, Nigeria,London, Germany,Quatar,Serbia, Switzerland and Rwanda. Through his vibrant and thought-provoking art, David not only celebrate the beauty and culture of Rwanda but also makes significant contributions to the grobal art dialogue. His works continues to inspire and engage audiences world wide, bringing cultural and artistic boundaries.

Artist Statement

As a mixed media artist, I draw inspiration from the vibrant tapestry of everyday life and the breathtaking landscapes of Rwanda. My artistic journey began at a young age in nursery school, Later it grew as i joined nyundo art school, where I honed a diversity of techniques, particularly in ballpoint pen. I believe that mistakes in art can transform into powerful designs, a philosophy that has shaped my creative process. Throughout my career, I've participated in various art competitions, including the Rwanda Transparency Art Competition and Art Rwanda Ubuhanzi, allowing me to share my work on both local and international stages. My exhibitions in countries such as Belgium, France, and the USA have provided platforms to engage with diverse audiences and contribute to global art dialogues. My art serves as a tribute to Rwanda’s rich heritage and hardworking people. Each piece tells a story, capturing the essence of Rwandan society and celebrating its beauty. Collaborating with NGOs like UNHCR and UNESCO has further deepened my commitment to using art as a means of connection and reflection. I strive to create work that resonates with viewers, fostering a deeper appreciation for our shared human experiences and the cultural nuances that define us. Through my vibrant and thought-provoking pieces, I aim to bridge cultural boundaries and inspire others to see the world through a lens of creativity and understanding.
